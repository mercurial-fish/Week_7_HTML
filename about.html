About Me and Spoon Fed

Hello! And welcome to Spoon Fed: A Queer Parents' Guide to Stuff You Can Watch With Your Kids. I am your host, Dezi Cruz.
For those of you just tuning in, let me tell you a little bit about me. I am a queer, non-binary, neurodivergent parent of two kids.
They are 6 and 8 years old, so I've got a few years under my belt. And in my personal experience, due to limited time,
limited emotional bandwidth, and the million other things that make it hard to exist as a human being, I have found that my kids and I
can really connect when we watch TV/Movies that we all enjoy. Even if I can't sit down to watch it start to finish with them,
I'm more likely to be engaged with what they're doing and watching if it's something I also find interesting. And then it gives us things
we can discuss or talk about. It also gives me an opportunity to point out some potentially problematic things, because, let's face it,
anything made before 2015 is probably gonna have some transphobic shit in it. And not just that, but racist or sexist or homophobic.
Doesn't mean the whole show or movie is bad, but it's something that is good to point out, so they don't internalize that stuff as normal.
Butttt it's hard for me to catch any of that stuff if I am so bored with what they're watching that I've tuned it out.

So long story short, my aim here is to help queer parents connect with their kids by giving movie and TV suggestions that everyone will truly enjoy,
along with some talking points to help get the ball rolling.

Why should you listen?

Because I have taken out the guesswork! In about 20 minutes, you'll have an overview of the movie and talking points,
so all you have to do is decide whether or not you want to watch it with your kids.

Where can you start?

You don't need to start from the very beginning or go in order.
Just because I like all of these movies, doesn't mean you do. And I
know there may be certain types of movies you're vehemently opposed to.
